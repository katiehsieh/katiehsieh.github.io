Musical Instruments

Strings

String instruments are usually bowed, plucked, strummed, or struck to produce the sound.

Examples

  • violin
  • viola
  • cello
  • bass
  • guitar
  • ukulele
Full list

String instruments have different lengths and thicknesses of stretched strings. Longer and looser strings produce lower notes, while shorter and tighter strings produce higher notes. The strings also produce different sounds based on what material they are made of, such as nylon or steel.

Common string instruments that use a bow include the violin, viola, cello. The bow is a stick with hair attached to it, and the bow slides against the strings to make the sound. These bowed instruments are most often found in orchestras. The guitar, ukulele, and harp are some instruments that are usually plucked and strummed.

Most brass instruments produce different notes by pressing down valves that change the length of the tubing that the air passes through. Others, notably the trombone, produce different notes with a slide. Brass instruments also come in different sizes and have different lenghts of tubing. The smaller instruments can produce higher notes, while the larger instruments produce the lower notes.

The trumpet and french horn are relatively smaller brass instruments that can play higher notes. The trombone, baritone, and euphonium are medium sized. The tuba is the largest brass instrument and plays the lowest bass notes in an ensmemble. These breass instruments are often found in orchestras, concert bands, and jazz bands.

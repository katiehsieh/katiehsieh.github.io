Musical Instruments

Keyboard

Keyboard instruments have a set of keys or levers pressed by fingers to produce the sound.

Examples

  • piano
  • harpsichord
  • celesta
  • organ
  • accordian
  • synthesizer
Full list

Each key on a keyboard instrument corresponds to a single pitch. The keyboard includes white keys (for the natural notes) and black keys (for the sharps and flats). There are a variety of ways keyboard instruments produce the notes that are played. Keyboard instruments are found in many musical genres and ensembles and make a variety of different sounds.

The piano and harpsichord have hammers that hit strings inside of the instrument when the key is pressed. The pipe organ and accordian produce the sounds by pushing air through the instrument. Electric keyboards and synthesizers produce the sounds through electronic or digital signals through an amplifier.
